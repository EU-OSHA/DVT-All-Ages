Healthy life years vs. life expectancy

Life expectancy and healthy life years (HLY) expectancy, reflecting health and functional status of population, have implications for possibilities to extend working lives. Healthy life years’ expectancy hasn’t increased to the same extent as life expectancy – we live longer, but not necessarily healthier.

In addition, there are great differences between the countries. Life expectancy also varies by educational status. Throughout the EU people with lower education, a lower occupational class or lower income tend to die younger and have a higher incidence of most types of health problems. Workplace exposures are one of a wide range of factors that affect health and life expectancy.
